If something brought you to this page — a feeling that the system you're in wasn't built for you, that you're working harder and harder to stay still, that your creativity is being harvested but your life isn't improving — you're probably right.

The good news: the tools to leave are free. Most of them have always been free. The barrier isn't money. It's knowing they exist and having someone say: it's okay to use them.

It's okay to use them.

Reclaim your phone

De-Google your Android
GrapheneOS turns a Pixel phone into a privacy-respecting device. No Google Play Services by default. Free. Open source. The gold standard.
grapheneos.org
Alternative app store
F-Droid is a free and open source Android app repository. No tracking. No ads. Apps that respect you.
f-droid.org
Privacy browser
Firefox with uBlock Origin blocks the surveillance layer. Brave is another strong option. Both free.
firefox.com · brave.com

Reclaim your identity online

Portable social identity (Bluesky)
Your followers travel with you if you move servers. Your identity isn't owned by the platform. AT Protocol.
bsky.app
Federated social (Mastodon)
No algorithm. No ads. Thousands of servers, anyone can run one. Your account on one server connects to all others.
joinmastodon.org
Your own page (FeelFamous hamlet)
60 seconds to create. Free forever. Your own URL. No account needed. Just a name and a craft. A place to exist online that nobody can delete.
feelfamous.co.uk

Reclaim your income

Coffeeware model
Build something useful. Make the core features free forever. Let people donate if they want to. The service never degrades if they can't. This is a complete business model. It works.
Desire paths
Watch where people walk. Then pave it. The best products are discovered, not invented. Build what people are already trying to do badly.
The FeelFamous model
Free community village. Members get their own page. Donations optional. Revenue from trust, not extraction. Proven at scale in under two weeks, with no budget.

Reclaim your movement

1992. Hitchhiking. A man with a towel tied with string. Inside: a toothbrush and a credit card. I had a 65-litre rucksack.

I spent 30 years becoming the towel guy.

The freedom isn't in the toothbrush. It's in the decision that you don't need more than the toothbrush.

Travel light. The things that weigh you down physically are usually metaphors for the things that weigh you down mentally. Start with one bag. Work backwards from there.

España: Teléfono de la Esperanza 717 003 717 (gratuito, 24/7)
